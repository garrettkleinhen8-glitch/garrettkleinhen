Senior Capstone – Varible Vane Test Rig

Designed and built a Pratt and Whitney Sponsored mechanical test rig for evaluating moment gengerated on Varrible Vanes.

  • Designed and developed a custom test rig in SolidWorks to measure angular moment on a variable vane system
  • Performed structural loading analysis on 3D printed components to ensure integrity under wind tunnel forces
  • Integrated a strain gauge-based sensing system to capture torque-induced deformation
  • Conducted wind tunnel testing across varying flow velocities and vane angles

PLA Recycler

As a part of a Mechnatronics class our team Designed and Built a PLA Recyler to reuse scrap material promoting less waste from 3D printing.

  • Designed and built a low-cost PLA recycling system to reduce 3D printing scrap in the university makerspace.
  • Developed a thermal extrusion system using a heating element to melt shredded PLA within a controlled tube assembly.
  • Integrated a motor-driven auger mechanism to convey and pressurize molten plastic through a custom nozzle.

Auto Pet Feeder

As a part of a Junior Design Project class our team Designed and Built an Automatic Pet Feeder to help busy pet parent make sure their pets are fed if they cannot be home.

  • Designed and built a web-controlled automatic dog feeder with portion-controlled dispensing with scheduling.
  • Developed a motor-driven dispensing wheel with precision-sized cavities to deliver exactly ¼ cup of food per rotation
  • Engineered lid-opening mechanism that activates only when authorized RFID tag is detected within defined proximity

Milling Project - Micrometer Stand

As a part of a Manufacting class our team Designed and Built a micrometer stand from raw materials.

  • Interpreted the micrometer stand plans to determine dimensions, features, tolerances, and the machining sequence.
  • Machined flat surfaces, drilled/tapped holes, and cut slots on the base and mounting components.
  • Turned cylindrical posts/rods, faced ends, drilled axial holes, and added threads where needed.

Automatic Beverage Pourer

As a part of a Mechanisms class final I Designed and Built a automatic beverage pooer where we use our new found mechanism design skills.

  • Mapped the main motion trajectory to guide the mechanism’s design and Created the mechanism’s individual links in SolidWorks based on the mapped path
  • Built a 2D version to test and validated the motion before full modeling, using 3D printing
  • Assembled the full 3D printed model of the pouring mechanism adding a full motorized geared system
